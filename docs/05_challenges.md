# Hardest Problems, Challenges, and Tradeoffs

## Overview
Some of the most interesting parts of Justin’s background come from the difficult tradeoffs he has had to manage rather than from any single isolated accomplishment. He has often worked in environments where there was no perfect answer, only better and worse decisions under uncertainty.

## Signature Challenge Theme
A signature challenge in Justin’s work has been balancing growth, customer experience, and portfolio discipline at the same time. In lending and risk-related settings, these goals can easily conflict:
- improving approvals can increase risk
- tightening controls can reduce customer adoption
- optimizing short-term metrics can hurt long-term economics
- moving quickly can create governance or execution problems

Justin’s strength has been helping organizations reason clearly through those tensions rather than oversimplifying them.

## Challenging Experience at Amazon
A strong employer-facing answer to the question "What was your most challenging experience at Amazon?" is:

One of the most challenging parts of Justin’s Amazon experience was helping lead in an environment where growth, risk, economics, governance, and execution speed all had to be balanced at once. The challenge was not simply technical. It required judgment, disciplined measurement, strong stakeholder management, and the ability to keep teams focused on durable outcomes rather than short-term signals.

## Why These Situations Were Hard
These situations were difficult because they involved:
- incomplete information
- multiple stakeholders with legitimate but competing priorities
- significant consequences for getting the decision wrong
- the need for fast action without losing rigor
- ambiguity that could not be solved by a model alone

## How Justin Approaches Hard Problems
Justin typically approaches hard problems by:
1. Clarifying the actual decision to be made.
2. Identifying the core tradeoffs rather than debating secondary noise.
3. Defining what success and failure would look like.
4. Using data and experimentation where possible.
5. Being explicit about uncertainty and assumptions.
6. Recommending a path that is both analytically credible and operationally executable.

## Example Themes for Behavioral Interviews
Potential behavioral stories can be framed around:
- balancing growth and risk under pressure
- improving a flawed measurement approach
- aligning multiple stakeholders around a better decision framework
- leading through ambiguity when no function owned the full problem
- building mechanisms that reduced repeat decision friction
- choosing long-term health over attractive but misleading short-term metrics

## Failure and Learning Narrative
Justin is comfortable talking about the reality that hard problems rarely resolve cleanly. One of his strengths is learning from imperfect decisions and using those lessons to improve future mechanisms, measurement, and team judgment. He tends to view setbacks as opportunities to improve the quality of how decisions are made, not just the quality of a single outcome.

## Public-Safe Employer Answer Bank
### Q: What was your hardest leadership challenge?
A: One of the hardest leadership challenges was guiding teams through high-stakes tradeoffs where speed, rigor, stakeholder alignment, and long-term business health all mattered. The challenge was making sure the organization did not confuse activity or short-term gains with durable value creation.

### Q: What kind of ambiguity do you handle well?
A: Justin handles ambiguity well when the problem spans multiple functions and the right answer requires both analytical depth and business judgment. He is particularly effective when success depends on framing the problem correctly before trying to optimize it.

### Q: What makes your work difficult to do well?
A: The work is difficult because the real challenge is often not technical complexity alone. It is the combination of tradeoffs, execution constraints, stakeholder dynamics, and the need to make good decisions before perfect information is available.
