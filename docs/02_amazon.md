# Amazon Experience

## Role Summary
At Amazon, Justin led work at the intersection of consumer lending, credit risk, analytics, and data science. His role involved helping shape strategy, measurement, and operating mechanisms for lending-related decisions while partnering across product, finance, engineering, operations, and senior leadership.

## Scope
Justin’s Amazon experience can be described as a mix of:
- risk and lending strategy
- data science leadership
- experimentation and measurement
- portfolio and customer decision optimization
- governance-conscious execution in a complex environment
- org building and cross-functional influence

He operated in a context where decisions had to account for multiple competing goals at once, including:
- customer experience
- growth and adoption
- unit economics and long-term profitability
- loss performance and portfolio health
- model governance and control expectations
- operational feasibility and stakeholder alignment

## Nature of the Work
Justin’s work was not limited to building models. A major part of the role involved designing decision frameworks that could be used repeatedly and at scale. That included:
- defining what success should mean
- deciding which tradeoffs mattered most
- ensuring metrics reflected the real business objective
- aligning teams around disciplined experimentation
- helping leaders make informed decisions under uncertainty

He was especially effective in roles where technical analysis had to drive real operating choices rather than remain purely theoretical.

## Key Contributions
Examples of the kinds of contributions Justin made at Amazon include:
- building more rigorous approaches to lending and risk decisioning
- improving the way experiments were framed and interpreted
- helping the organization reason about incrementality rather than relying on superficial win metrics
- creating clearer mechanisms for balancing approval, risk, and economic outcomes
- raising the quality of cross-functional decision-making through better analytical framing
- developing teams and leaders who could operate effectively at higher scope

## Leadership and Influence
Justin’s Amazon work required influence well beyond his direct reporting line. He had to earn trust across technical and non-technical stakeholders and communicate complex issues in a way that supported good executive decisions.

This included:
- presenting nuanced tradeoffs clearly
- advocating for disciplined, evidence-based decisions
- aligning different functions around common objectives
- helping teams avoid overreacting to incomplete data
- balancing urgency with rigor

## Most Challenging Experience
One of the most challenging aspects of Justin’s Amazon experience was balancing growth with risk in a high-scale lending environment. In practice, that meant managing tension between:
- driving customer adoption and approvals
- maintaining healthy loss outcomes
- preserving long-term economics
- satisfying governance and control expectations
- moving fast enough to support the business while staying analytically disciplined

What made this especially difficult was that these goals were often in conflict. The role demanded judgment, not just analysis. Justin had to help the organization distinguish between short-term gains and durable value creation, while also making sure teams used credible measurement methods.

## Why This Experience Matters
Justin’s Amazon background is relevant to senior leadership roles because it demonstrates more than technical skill. It shows an ability to:
- lead through ambiguity
- make high-stakes tradeoffs
- operate across disciplines
- scale teams and mechanisms
- bring rigor to environments where simple answers are rarely enough

## Public-Safe Framing for Employers
In employer-facing settings, Justin typically describes his Amazon work in a sanitized and strategic way. He focuses on the nature of the problems, the scale of decision-making, the leadership dimension, and the tradeoffs involved, while avoiding confidential internal details.
