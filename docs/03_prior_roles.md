# Prior Roles and Career Progression

## Career Arc
Justin’s career progression reflects increasing scope across analytics, decision-making, risk-oriented problem solving, and leadership. Over time, his responsibilities evolved from analytical and strategy-heavy work into broader leadership roles that required organizational design, stakeholder management, and ownership of complex business outcomes.

## Common Through-Line Across Roles
Even as titles and contexts changed, several themes remained consistent:
- solving difficult decision problems with data
- building credibility through rigor and clarity
- operating in environments where judgment mattered as much as technical skill
- working across business and technical stakeholders
- growing into larger leadership scope over time

## What Earlier Roles Prepared Him To Do
Justin’s earlier career helped build the foundation for his later leadership roles by strengthening his ability to:
- break down complex business questions
- define useful metrics and decision frameworks
- identify the difference between signal and noise
- influence cross-functional partners without formal authority
- connect analytical work to financial and operating impact

## Career Progression Narrative
A useful way to explain Justin’s progression is:
1. He developed strong analytical and strategic problem-solving skills.
2. He took on increasingly complex business questions tied to risk, growth, and operating performance.
3. He expanded from solving problems directly to building teams and mechanisms that could solve them repeatedly at scale.
4. He became a leader responsible not only for analysis quality, but for organizational effectiveness, talent development, and executive alignment.
